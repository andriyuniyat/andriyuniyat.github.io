Automating identity lifecycle management to reduce access risk

Designing joiner–mover–leaver workflows that scale securely across SaaS platforms.

Context

Manual onboarding and offboarding processes increase the risk of orphaned accounts, excessive access, and audit findings. I was responsible for designing and improving identity lifecycle workflows to make access changes predictable, auditable, and scalable.

My role

  • Owned identity lifecycle design across SaaS applications
  • Integrated HR-driven events with identity and access workflows
  • Partnered with Security and IT to reduce manual access handling

Approach

  • Joiner: standardized onboarding using group-based access and identity-driven provisioning
  • Mover: aligned role changes with access updates to prevent privilege creep
  • Leaver: ensured timely deprovisioning and access removal across connected systems
  • Fallback controls: documented manual processes where automation was not technically possible
  • Operational visibility: created documentation and checkpoints for access changes

Constraints & tradeoffs

  • Not all SaaS platforms supported full automation or SCIM provisioning
  • Identity workflows needed to accommodate edge cases without becoming brittle
  • Security controls had to align with HR and business timelines

Outcomes

  • Reduced orphaned accounts and delayed access removal through standardized workflows
  • Lowered operational overhead by minimizing manual access changes
  • Improved audit confidence by making access lifecycle steps consistent and documentable
  • Created a repeatable identity pattern reusable across future SaaS integrations

What I’d do next

  • Expand automation coverage as vendors mature their identity capabilities
  • Introduce periodic lifecycle audits to catch edge cases and drift

Note: This write-up is intentionally sanitized and does not include user data, system identifiers, or internal workflows.
