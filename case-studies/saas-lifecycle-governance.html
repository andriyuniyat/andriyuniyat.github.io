Governing enterprise SaaS platforms across the full lifecycle

From onboarding and access control to renewals, audits, and long-term ownership.

Context

As organizations scale, SaaS platforms often accumulate without clear ownership, governance standards, or lifecycle discipline. This creates security risk, audit gaps, operational friction, and renewal surprises. I was responsible for governing multiple enterprise SaaS platforms across their full lifecycle in a regulated environment.

My role

  • Owned SaaS platform governance from onboarding through renewal and decommissioning
  • Defined access, identity integration, and ownership standards for business applications
  • Acted as a technical partner to Security, Legal, HR, Finance, and IT leadership

Approach

  • Ownership clarity: established clear technical and business owners for each SaaS platform
  • Access governance: aligned identity integration, provisioning, and access review requirements
  • Vendor alignment: partnered with Security and Legal on risk assessments, contract language, and renewals
  • Operational continuity: documented platform dependencies, renewal dates, and runbooks
  • Lifecycle thinking: treated SaaS as long-lived systems, not one-time implementations

Constraints & tradeoffs

  • Different vendors provided inconsistent security and identity capabilities
  • Governance needed to scale without becoming a blocker to business adoption
  • Documentation had to be thorough yet usable by multiple teams

Outcomes

  • Reduced long-term risk by clarifying ownership and governance expectations across platforms
