SSO & access governance across 10+ SaaS applications

Identity architecture patterns focused on secure access, automation, and audit readiness in a regulated environment.

Context

Multiple business-critical SaaS applications had inconsistent onboarding/offboarding steps, uneven access controls, and limited visibility during audit cycles. The goal was to reduce friction for users while strengthening governance and accountability.

My role

  • Platform owner for SaaS access patterns and identity integration standards
  • Partnered with Security, HR, Legal, and IT stakeholders on governance requirements
  • Owned implementation approach, documentation, and operational handoffs

Approach

  • Standardized SSO patterns using Azure AD / Entra ID with SAML/OIDC based on vendor capabilities
  • Provisioning strategy using SCIM where supported; otherwise defined safe, controlled alternatives
  • Access governance: mapped roles/groups to least-privilege access models and created review checkpoints
  • Operational readiness: built repeatable runbooks for certificate/metadata renewals and access troubleshooting

Outcomes

  • Reduced manual account management through consistent group-based access patterns
  • Improved audit readiness with clearer ownership, access review practices, and documentation
  • Decreased onboarding/offboarding risk by removing “one-off” access methods
  • Lowered support friction with standardized troubleshooting steps and clear escalation paths

What I’d do next

  • Expand access review cadence and automate reporting where possible
  • Continuously harden conditional access posture and exception handling
  • Build a single “access model” catalog for all SaaS apps to simplify governance

Note: This write-up is intentionally generalized and does not include internal identifiers, vendor contracts, or proprietary configurations.
